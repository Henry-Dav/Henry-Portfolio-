What I noticed

  • Opened with what the product imagines, not with the reader.
  • The benefits were abstract — "adapts to your unique thinking style" is hard to picture.
  • The same button asked three times before there was a reason to press it.

What changed

  • Started where the reader actually is: skeptical, already using something else.
  • Swapped the abstract promises for prompts they can paste immediately.
  • One ask instead of three: pick one and try it.
