What I noticed

  • Reported the app's progress, not the reader's reason for signing up.
  • Features were listed in one sentence, with no moment attached to them.
  • The only thing to do was visit a website that isn't the app yet.

What changed

  • Started with the woods — the thing they actually signed up for.
  • Tied each feature to the kid using it.
  • Gave them something to do while they wait: follow along.

What I noticed

  • Buried the one thing that decides everything: it's iPhone only.
  • The features sat inside a long sentence, so they were easy to skim past.
  • Feedback was asked for politely, without saying what it's for.

What changed

  • Said it's iOS only in the third line, so nobody reads the steps for nothing.
  • Pulled the features out into their own list.
  • Gave feedback a reason: it shapes what comes next.
